# Rahul's Journey — A Therapy Story

## The People

**Rahul Kapoor** (28, software engineer) — Feels anxious all the time. Can't sleep. His wife Priya pushed him to get help. He finally signed up for Lumio.

**Priya Kapoor** (27, teacher) — Rahul's wife. She noticed him spiraling for months — not sleeping, snapping at small things, avoiding social events. She wants to support his recovery.

**Dr. Ananya Sharma** (therapist) — Specializes in CBT (Cognitive Behavioral Therapy). She helps Rahul understand his thought patterns and teaches him coping techniques.

**Dr. Vikram Mehta** (psychiatrist) — Prescribes and manages medication. He puts Rahul on Sertraline (an SSRI antidepressant commonly used for anxiety).

**Anita Desai** (care coordinator) — Makes sure everything runs smoothly. Schedules appointments, follows up, connects Rahul with the right people.

---

## Week 1: The Beginning

Rahul downloads Lumio after a particularly bad night — 2 hours of sleep, racing thoughts about a work deadline, chest tightness. He fills out the intake form: "I feel anxious all the time. I can't sleep. I don't enjoy anything anymore."

He's matched with Dr. Sharma (therapist), Dr. Mehta (psychiatrist), and Anita (coordinator).

### Session 1 — Intake (60 min)

Dr. Sharma talks to Rahul. He opens up about:
- **Excessive worry** about work, finances, and health
- **Insomnia** — takes 2+ hours to fall asleep, wakes at 3am
- **Rumination** — replays conversations, imagines worst-case scenarios

She administers two assessments:
- **PHQ-9** (depression scale): Score **18/27** — moderately severe depression
- **GAD-7** (anxiety scale): Score **15/21** — severe anxiety

**Diagnosis:** Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD), ICD-10 code F41.1, moderate severity.

### Medication Prescribed

Dr. Mehta prescribes **Sertraline 50mg** (brand name: Zoloft), an SSRI. Take once daily in the morning. Common side effects: nausea, headache, drowsiness.

### Treatment Plan (v1)

Goals:
1. Reduce GAD-7 score below 10
2. Sleep 7+ hours consistently
3. Reduce PHQ-9 score below 10

Interventions:
- Weekly CBT sessions with Dr. Sharma
- Sertraline 50mg daily
- Sleep hygiene education

---

## Week 2: Session 2 — Starting CBT

Rahul returns. Mood: 3/10 before, 5/10 after session.

Dr. Sharma introduces CBT — explaining how thoughts, feelings, and behaviors are connected. She teaches the concept of "cognitive distortions" — patterns of thinking that make anxiety worse:
- **Catastrophizing**: "If I miss this deadline, I'll get fired, lose my house, my marriage will fail"
- **Mind reading**: "My boss thinks I'm incompetent"

Rahul nods. He recognizes himself in these patterns.

---

## Week 3: Session 3 — Sleep Problems Worsen

Rahul looks exhausted. He's only sleeping 2-3 hours a night. The Sertraline hasn't kicked in yet (takes 4-6 weeks for full effect).

Dr. Sharma teaches **sleep hygiene**:
- No screens 1 hour before bed
- Same bedtime every night
- No caffeine after 2pm

Topic discussed: work deadline pressure — a major project is due and Rahul is convinced he'll fail.

---

## Week 4: Session 4 — The Thought Record

Mood before: 4/10. Mood after: 6/10.

Dr. Sharma introduces the **Thought Record** technique:
1. Write down the anxious thought
2. Identify the cognitive distortion
3. Write a more balanced alternative thought

Example from Rahul:
- Thought: "I'm going to miss the deadline and get fired"
- Distortion: Catastrophizing
- Balanced: "I've met every deadline so far. Even if this one slips, my track record is solid."

Rahul is assigned homework: complete 3 thought records before next session.

**Insight discovered:** Work deadlines consistently trigger Rahul's anxiety spirals. This is a recurring pattern.

---

## Between Sessions: Rahul Journals

Two days after Session 4, Rahul can't sleep at 2am. He opens the Lumio app and writes:

> "Can't stop thinking about tomorrow's client presentation. I know Dr. Sharma said to do a thought record but my mind is racing too fast. I feel like I'm drowning. Priya is asleep and I don't want to wake her."

**Mood log:** mood=low, energy=3/10, sleep_hours=3.5

---

## Week 5: Session 5 — First Signs of Progress

Mood before: 5/10. Mood after: 7/10.

Rahul completed 2 out of 3 thought records (difficulty: 5/10, helpfulness: 7/10). He says: "The first one was hard but by the second one, I caught myself catastrophizing in real-time."

Sleep is slightly better — 4-5 hours now instead of 2-3. The Sertraline is starting to work.

**Insight:** Rahul is developing metacognition — he's starting to observe his own thought patterns instead of being controlled by them.

---

## Week 6: Monthly Assessment

Rahul retakes the PHQ-9.
- **PHQ-9 score: 14/27** (was 18) — moderate depression. 4-point improvement.
- **GAD-7 score: 12/21** (was 15) — moderate anxiety. 3-point improvement.

Treatment is working. Both scores trending down.

---

## Week 8: Session 8 — Setback

Dr. Mehta increased Sertraline from 50mg to 100mg two weeks ago (to accelerate progress).

**Problem:** Rahul now feels nauseous every morning. Sleep regressed to 3 hours. He says: "I was getting better and now I feel worse. What's the point?"

New symptoms:
- **Nausea** — daily, mild, every morning (likely Sertraline side effect)
- **Hopelessness** — "What's the point of treatment?" (moderate severity)

Dr. Sharma flags the hopelessness as a **risk indicator** — not suicidal ideation, but passive hopelessness that needs monitoring.

She consults with Dr. Mehta about the side effects. They decide to:
1. Monitor nausea for 2 more weeks (often subsides)
2. Add anti-nausea strategy (take Sertraline with food)
3. Increase session frequency if hopelessness persists

**Insight:** Sleep regression correlates with the Sertraline dose increase. Causal chain: dose increase → nausea → disrupted sleep → hopelessness.

### Treatment Plan Updated (v2)

Goals (revised):
1. Reduce GAD-7 below 10
2. Sleep 7+ hours consistently
3. Manage medication side effects
4. Monitor hopelessness

Interventions (revised):
- Weekly CBT sessions (consider twice-weekly if needed)
- Sertraline 100mg with food
- Sleep hygiene + relaxation techniques
- Side effect monitoring with Dr. Mehta
